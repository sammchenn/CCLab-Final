YIP YIP

Scientific Name:

Obesius Porcinus Giganticus

Discovered by / at:

Dr. Percius McSnoutworth at the Swine University in Porcellino, Philadelphia

Family:

This pig may look like an ordinary pig, however, Obesius Porcinus Giganticus is a specific pig species born immensely round. Their diet consists of strictly apples. They are an endangered species due to being constantly slaughtered since they make very delicous bacon.

Habitat:

This species is born in a mud pile and they spend the rest of their says roaming about mud and barren land in search of apples.

Appearance:

Obesius Porcinus Giganticus has the qualities of a normal pig: pink color, hooves, etc. however, has an appetite for red apples and born round. They are born with only two legs, so they walk like humans.

Key Characteristics:

  • Apple lover.
  • Round.
  • Keen sense of smell
  • Oddly picky

What we know so far about Obesius Porcinus Giganticus Obesius Porcinus Giganticus has been descended from Sus Scrofa Domesticus. Their history dates back to 10,000 years ago when pigs were first discovered. As pigs have evolved and been domesticated, Obesius Porcinus Giganticus no longer feels threatened by outside predators and their natural body has become slightly larger. They have not adjusted to humans being predators, however, so they are extremely friendly. While they can hunt for apples on their own, they especially love being hand fed apples.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Feel free to feed YIP YIP. YIP YIP is extremely friendly, but is oddly specific with the amount of apples she eats. She has to eat a certain amount of apples within 15 seconds to be full. However, that amount changes every day based upon how she feels. If you don't feed her the correct amount of apples within 15 seconds, she dies. If you overfeed her, she also dies. If you are able to correctly feed her the right amount of apples within 15 seconds apples, she will live and be super happy!
